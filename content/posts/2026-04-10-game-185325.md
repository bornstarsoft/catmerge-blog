In a shocking revelation, Gunzilla Games, the blockchain game development studio co-founded by acclaimed director Neill Blomkamp, is facing serious allegations from former employees regarding unpaid wages. Reports indicate that some staff members have not received their salaries for several months, raising significant concerns about the studio's financial practices and treatment of its workforce.

Former animator Paul Creamer, who worked at Gunzilla from March 2025 to March 2026, publicly shared his experience, stating he hasn't been paid since October 2025. He claims that despite assurances from upper management about resolving payment delays, many employees continue to work without compensation. This situation has sparked outrage among current and former staff, with multiple individuals coming forward to share similar stories of unpaid wages and attempts to silence their complaints.

The allegations against Gunzilla Games highlight a troubling trend in the gaming industry, particularly within emerging blockchain sectors. As the demand for innovative gaming experiences grows, so does the potential for exploitation of creative talent. The question remains: how can the industry ensure fair treatment and accountability for its workforce in the face of such challenges?
