Sanrio, the beloved creator of Hello Kitty, is venturing into the gaming world with its new brand, Sanrio Games. This strategic move aims to tap into the rapidly growing global gaming market by integrating its iconic characters into a series of party games for Nintendo Switch and Switch 2. With plans to release ten titles over the next three years, this initiative not only reflects a broader trend among Japanese IP owners but also showcases Sanrio's commitment to diversifying its revenue streams through interactive entertainment.

The first game is set to launch this fall, allowing players to create their own avatars in a vibrant town filled with Sanrio characters. This approach is reminiscent of successful strategies employed by peers like Bandai Namco and Nintendo, who have long leveraged their character assets across various media. As Sanrio's CEO Tomokuni Tsuji noted, the gaming sector presents a unique opportunity to expand their character portfolio into the North American market, which could significantly enhance their licensing business in the future.

While the initial investment of around 10 billion yen indicates a bold commitment, it's important to recognize that the standalone gaming business may face challenges, including anticipated losses. However, if Sanrio can successfully harness its rich character portfolio to create engaging titles, it could pave the way for a new growth driver in the company's future. Will Sanrio Games become a beloved part of the gaming landscape, or will it struggle to find its footing? Only time will tell.
