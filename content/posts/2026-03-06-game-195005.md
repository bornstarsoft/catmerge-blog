Capcom has made an exciting announcement by moving up the release date of its highly anticipated sci-fi action game, Pragmata, to April 17. This change not only gives fans a chance to dive into the game a week earlier than expected but also positions it just ahead of PlayStation's upcoming title, Saros, set for April 30. While the reason for the date change remains undisclosed, it certainly adds to the excitement surrounding Pragmata's launch.

Pragmata promises a unique gameplay experience, blending action with a hacking twist. Players will need to multitask between shooting robotic enemies and hacking their defenses through engaging puzzle mini-games. Set in a near-future lunar research station, the game follows protagonists Hugh and his android companion, Diana, as they navigate through a chilling environment filled with challenges. Early impressions from media previews have praised the game for its polished feel and intriguing concepts, leaving many eager for its full release.

As we count down to the new release date, one can't help but wonder how Pragmata will innovate within the gaming landscape. Will it live up to the high expectations set by Capcom's track record? Only time will tell, but the anticipation is certainly building among gamers worldwide.
