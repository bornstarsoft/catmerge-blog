Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick recently shed light on Rockstar Games' strategy of releasing titles like Grand Theft Auto 6 on consoles before PC. This approach, he argues, is rooted in the desire to serve the "core consumer" first, ensuring that the primary audience is prioritized in the gaming experience. Historically, this method has proven effective for Rockstar, as they focus on delivering the best possible experience to console players before considering PC ports.

Zelnick's comments highlight a significant shift in the gaming landscape, where PC sales for major titles have surged from a mere 5% to potentially 50%. Despite this growth, Rockstar remains committed to its console-first philosophy, emphasizing that the core consumer's needs must be met before branching out to other platforms. This strategy not only reinforces brand loyalty but also sets the stage for a more polished PC release in the future.

As we look ahead, it raises the question: will this console-first approach continue to dominate the industry, or will we see a shift towards simultaneous releases across platforms? The gaming community is eager to find out.
